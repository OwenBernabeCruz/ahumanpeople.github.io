Uganda: 1947

The Zika Virus was discovered in Uganda 1947 and it was common in Africa and Asia. The virus started to spread in 2013, but it was confirmed as deadly in 2015.

Where it's spread

The Zika Virus started in Uganda, but it's first outbreak was in 1952 and people have reported it in Tropical Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

Microcephaly

As the virus start developing, it started to affect child births. The virus can affect the babies head with a condition called microcephaly. The baby is born with an abnormally small head, and as a consequence the brain doesn't develop properly.

United States: 2015

The first ever Zika Virus outbreak in the United States happened in April 2015 and the health agencies warned people of the virus and advised people to bring bug repellent to foreign countries.

Zika Now

Today the Zika Virus is not relevant anymore, but it is still an important topic and in the near future, there will be a cure for this virus.
